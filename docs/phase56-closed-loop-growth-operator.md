# Phase 56: Closed-Loop Growth Operator

Ferocity's SEO and marketing wedge now has a production-oriented growth loop foundation instead of only draft generation.

## What changed

- Added attribution-ready growth sources and events for campaigns, service focus, city focus, leads, jobs, reviews, invoices, and revenue.
- Added content quality reviews so SEO drafts can be scored for usefulness, local relevance, originality, conversion clarity, and risk flags before publishing.
- Added a provider-ready publishing queue for website, Google Business Profile, Facebook, Instagram, LinkedIn, email, SMS, and manual workflows.
- Added review request workflows with negative-review interception state and future Google Business Profile readiness.
- Added follow-up workflows for stale lead recovery, estimate follow-up, callbacks, nurture, and invoice follow-up.
- Added unified operator timeline events so SEO, content, reviews, follow-ups, and revenue can share one history.
- Added growth operator insights for content quality, publishing consistency, lead recovery, review flow, conversion tracking, attribution, and SEO compounding.
- Added workspace feature entitlements for SaaS plan and usage readiness.

## Product intent

The growth side should not become spammy, low-quality, generic AI content. Ferocity should create a loop:

1. Draft useful local content from real business data.
2. Quality review and approve it.
3. Queue publishing safely.
4. Track leads, estimates, jobs, reviews, and revenue back to the source.
5. Use that closed-loop data to surface operational recommendations.

This is the path toward "AI growth and operations platform for service businesses," not just CRM software or an AI blog writer.

## Provider posture

No new external provider action is active. Publishing, SMS, email, GBP, and review workflows are provider-ready and audit-ready, but they remain manual/draft-oriented until credentials, scopes, and webhooks are connected.
